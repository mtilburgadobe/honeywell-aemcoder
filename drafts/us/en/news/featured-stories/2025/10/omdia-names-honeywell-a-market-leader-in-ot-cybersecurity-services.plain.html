Walk into almost any plant, refinery or building control room, and the story is the same. Cybersecurity teams in industrial and buildings environments are dealing with rising OT cyber risk while specialized personnel remain scarce, and although the stakes are higher, early threat signals can get lost in a growing volume of alerts. Omdia’s 2025-26 Operational Technology Cybersecurity Services Report reflects what many cybersecurity leaders already experience: only 36% of organizations feel “very prepared” with their in-house OT security teams while alert fatigue and noise management are cited as top concerns.

Industry Recognition of OT Cybersecurity Leadership

Omdia’s report ranks Honeywell a leader in OT cybersecurity services following a survey of 220 cybersecurity professionals who rated only providers they had worked with and scored them on likelihood to recommend and service quality. Honeywell received best-in-class ratings in four out of six cybersecurity quadrants including OT MSS, solution breadth, strategy and innovation, and market momentum; with OT platforms and vendor execution scoring top-tier accolades.

Why This Recognition Matters for You

  • It acknowledges the need for dedicated OT services and industry knowledge that address real challenges in the field.
  • It confirms the importance of guidance on regulatory obligations so that programs stay aligned with changing requirements.
  • It highlights the value of partnerships that can bridge IT and OT priorities to protect safety and business continuity.
